DREAM PROGRAM

As a result of Ms. Mihika Rajesh's initiative, the DREAMS chapter in Bengaluru was established in February 2017. She was joined by her friends Ms. Yashikaa Shekar, Ms. Milonee Sanghavi, Mr. Saurab Dheer, Ms. Anusha Nag, Ms. Avina D​​​​ubey, and a few other student volunteers from the Department of Psychology and School of Education, central campus, CHRIST (Deemed to be University). To plan and carry out the intervention programme, they were guided by Rev. Father Lijo Thomas, who had a vision and provided them with leadership, direction, and training.

A powerful and successful community-based working approach is used by LET US DREAM to bring people together and link community organisations. LET US DREAM supports, develops, and hones people's unrealized potential in the psycho-social leadership facets of life. Through its four distinct projects, LET US DREAM works to realise this vision through thorough research and ongoing evaluation

The organisational culture of LET US DREAM is based on the principles of St. Kuriakose Elias Chavara, who believed in the integral development of the individual and societal commitment. As people, we have so much to contribute to society. Each person's success builds upon and serves as a base for society's development. We see humanity as a single, interconnected family in which everyone is depending on one another for their individual and collective success. We want to DREAM together and grow together as a community as we take a more holistic approach to life.

what we do

New Strategies for learning

The three-year model of the DREAMS Program is specifically designed and utilized to help the less privileged and the underperforming middle school students in their holistic development – year one focuses on Personal development, year two on Interpersonal skills, and year three on Leadership skills.

Goal

The goal of this curriculum and the community is to provide the students with the knowledge and skills they need to develop into better versions of themselves and to influence not only their own cultures but also those around them.

MISSION AND VISION

Mission

  • Promote the hidden talents for the holistic success of youth and adults
  • Enhance collaborations for sustainable achievements of communities
  • develop social commitments and responsibilities with a global outlook 
  • Create diverse platform for oppurtunities and support system where unidentified/struggling indiviuals to be empowered

Core Values

  • Inclusive Collaboration
  • Passion for Service & Stewardship
  • Talent Development & Mutual Empowerment
  • Continues Commitment for Improvement & Innovation
  • Global Perspective and Social Commitment
